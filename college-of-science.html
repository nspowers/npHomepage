College of Science Website

North Greenville University

Delivering a medium that showcases the people and programs in the College of Science with meaningful design, informative copy, and student imagery.

 
Services: Analytics, Copywriting, Coding, Digital Asset Management, Graphic and Web Design
Deliverables: College of Science main pages, department webpages, faculty and staff directory, social media

Origin

The Collage of Science's existing webpages listed each program's goals and a short summary of the program. Program pages lacked copy and descriptive imagery reflective of the program’s strengths. The absence of contact details or a call to action left prospective applicants uncertain of their next step for inquiry.

Developing Program Objectives

I led a kick-off meeting with the College of Science faculty and Department Heads to introduce the project and assembled a feedback group of students, faculty, and staff to suggest features and evaluate design iterations. The project required compatibility with the university’s existing CMS and I discovered how to integrate new features and responsive design into the existing platform.

Organizing Resources

As I collected content and images from department heads, I needed a better technique to sort my program assets and provide a sustainable methodology for future contributors. I researched digital asset management and curated a digital content system. This science library enables user submissions, organizes content by date and keyword, and reduces duplication of effort by providing images to other university offices.

Using analytics and user feedback to examine visitor flow through the existing website identified bottlenecks and confusion. I principles of information design to construct an informative an engaging visitor experience.

A Mobile-First Approach

The university's CMS is a legacy system that positions navigation and other page elements in locations incompatible with mobile users. For example, page navigation was positioned below the content, which was frequently outside the device's viewport. To prevent a user from needing to scroll down to a feature he or she didn't know existed, I developed a custom navigation panel.

Old Page Navigation

The original CMS template uses a dropdown menu with difficult legibility. The position envelopes the page, obscures the university's logo, and covers the titles and images.

New Page Navigation

The new science pages repositions the university logo above the navigation. This repositioning opened up new opportunities to page design. I restyled the site-wide navigation in a tabbed interface with greater clarity and improved button hover states.

The new webpages improve the online marketing for the College of Science and connects prospective and current students to university resources. Photographs of students interacting in their programs provide visual context for the science offerings. Accessible information on course sequences, program tracks, and a contact form provides timely information. Additionally, the update enabled major-specific student organizations, the College's honors program, and other science units to put resources online.

Online Faculty & Staff Directory

New faculty bio pages include photograph, office location, contact information, bio, and vitae.

Course Information Brochure

An information section for each program provides visitors the option to download that program's guide.

A place for students

New pages showcase student talent. Testimonials explore why current students selected the University and what graduates are doing now. College clubs and organizations now have a University page to post updates and share their identities.

Site Update

In April, 2016, the University published a new website. Many of the images and content I contributed were incorporated into the new platform.
