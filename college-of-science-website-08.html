College of Science Website

North Greenville University

Delivering a medium that showcases the people and programs in the College of Science with meaningful design, informative copy, and student imagery.

 
Services: Analytics, Copywriting, Coding, Digital Asset Management, Graphic and Web Design
Deliverables: College of Science main pages, department webpages, faculty and staff directory, social media

Origin

The Collage of Science's existing webpages listed each program's goals and a short summary of the program. Program pages lacked copy and descriptive imagery reflective of the program’s strengths. The absence of contact details or a call to action left prospective applicants uncertain of their next step for inquiry.

Developing Program Objectives

I led a kick-off meeting with the College of Science faculty and Department Heads to introduce the project and assembled a feedback group of students, faculty, and staff to suggest features and evaluate design iterations. The project required compatibility with the university’s existing CMS and I discovered how to integrate new features and responsive design into the existing platform.

Organizing Resources

As I collected content and images from department heads, I needed a better technique to sort my program assets and provide a sustainable methodology for future contributors. I researched digital asset management and curated a digital content system. This science library enables user submissions, organizes content by date and keyword, and reduces duplication of effort by providing images to other university offices.

Using analytics and user feedback to examine visitor flow through the existing website identifed bottlenecks and confusion. I principles of information design to construct an informative an engaging visitor experience.

Design

A Mobile-First Approch

The university's CMS is a legacy system that positions navigation and other page elements in locations incompatable with mobile users. For example, page navigation was positioned below the content, which was frequently outside the device's viewport. To prevent a user from needing to scroll down to a feature he or she didn't know existed, I developed a custom navigation panel.

Project Management

I coordinated the collection of imagery for the project, including scheduling photo shoots of students, photographing events, and catagorizing submissions from admissions and athetic photographers. I also filmed and edited a promotional video narriated by the program's director.

Medium: Print

Tools:  Adobe After Effects, InDesign, Illustrator, Premier Pro, and Photoshop
